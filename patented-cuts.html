Patented Innovations

Cuts that exist nowhere else in the world.

Six patented innovations. Four decades of mastery. Every cut designed, engineered, and perfected by Bez Ambar in his Los Angeles atelier.

Patented · 1982

Quadrillion®
The Princess Cut

In 1982, Bez Ambar unveiled the Quadrillion® — a square brilliant-cut diamond with a geometry so revolutionary it ignited a global transformation in diamond design. The world came to know it as the Princess Cut.

Today, the Princess Cut is the second most popular diamond shape on earth, found on tens of millions of engagement rings worldwide. Every single one traces back to this invention — to one man, in one atelier, in Los Angeles.

The Quadrillion's 49-facet design maximizes brilliance while its square profile allows for virtually seamless channel settings — an impossibility with round cuts.

Patented · 2003

Blaze® Cut

A proprietary diamond frame cut engineered to expand the apparent size of a center stone while dramatically intensifying its fire. No other cutter in the world can produce this effect.

The Blaze Cut uses a patented arrow-shaped facet pattern that acts as a prism, drawing light from the surrounding diamonds and reflecting it back through the center stone. The result: a ring that appears significantly larger and more brilliant than its carat weight would suggest.

Available exclusively in Bez Ambar settings. Protected by U.S. patent.

Patented · 2015

Divine Cut®

A round brilliant reimagined from the ground up. The Divine Cut adds additional facets engineered for maximum dispersion — producing fire visible from across a room.

Where a traditional round brilliant has 57 facets, the Divine Cut's proprietary geometry creates light performance that surpasses conventional cuts in every measurable dimension: brilliance, fire, and scintillation.

Bez Ambar's most recent patent — and, many say, his most extraordinary achievement.

More Innovations

Six patents. One atelier.

Micro-Pavé

Bez Ambar pioneered the micro-pavé setting technique — diamonds set so closely they appear to float as a continuous surface of light.

Invisible Setting

An advanced channel technique that conceals all metal, allowing princess-cut diamonds to lock together in a seamless mosaic of brilliance.

Tension Setting

A diamond held by the pressure of the band itself — no prongs, no bezels. The stone appears to float in mid-air. Engineering as art.

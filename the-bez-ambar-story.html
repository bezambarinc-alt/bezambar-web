The Bez Ambar Story

Innovation in Diamond Cutting Since 1979

1979

Ambar Diamonds Founded

Bez Ambar founds Ambar Diamonds in Los Angeles, bringing his vision of innovative diamond cutting from Israel to America. This marks the beginning of a legacy that would transform the jewelry industry.

1982

The Quadrillion / Princess Cut

Bez Ambar invents the Quadrillion cut — later known worldwide as the Princess Cut. This square modified brilliant cut maximizes brilliance from a square shape, becoming the second most popular diamond cut in the world.

1985

DeBeers Diamonds Today Award

The ATW ring by Bez Ambar wins the prestigious DeBeers Diamonds Today Award, cementing his reputation in the fine jewelry world and recognizing his exceptional artistry and innovation.

1988

Invisible Setting Mastered

Bez Ambar perfects the invisible setting technique, allowing diamonds and gemstones to be set side by side with no visible metal between them — creating a seamless surface of light that showcases the brilliance of each stone.

1992

The Boundless Setting

Invention of the Boundless Setting — a proprietary technique that holds stones with minimal metal, maximizing the visible surface area of each stone. This revolutionary setting technique becomes a signature of Bez Ambar designs.

1999

Micro-Pavé Pioneered

Bez Ambar pioneers micro-pavé setting techniques, setting diamonds as small as 0.005 carats with extraordinary precision. This technique adds delicate sparkle and sophistication to pieces while requiring masterful craftsmanship.

2003

The Blaze Cut

Invention of the Blaze Cut diamond — a proprietary cut with 13 arrows of light visible through the table. A true revolution in diamond cutting that maximizes the unique light-play characteristics of exceptional diamonds.

2015

The Divine Cut

The latest innovation — the Divine Cut diamond, featuring a unique faceting pattern that creates unparalleled fire and brilliance. This cut represents the culmination of decades of expertise and refined understanding of light and diamond optics.

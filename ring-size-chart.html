Ring Size Chart

Finding your perfect ring size is essential for a comfortable fit. Use our guide below to measure your ring size at home, or visit us in Los Angeles for a professional fitting.

Have questions? Call us at 213-629-9191 and our team will be happy to assist you.

How to Measure Your Ring Size at Home

Method 1: Measure an Existing Ring

  1. Find a ring that fits the intended finger comfortably.
  2. Measure the inside diameter (the distance from one inner edge to the other) in millimeters.
  3. Match the measurement to the chart below to find your size.

Method 2: Use a String or Paper Strip

  1. Cut a thin strip of paper or string and wrap it around the base of the ring finger.
  2. Mark the point where the strip overlaps.
  3. Measure the length in millimeters with a ruler.
  4. Match the circumference to the chart below to find your size.

Tips for an Accurate Measurement

  • Measure your finger over the knuckle to ensure the ring slides on comfortably.
  • Fingers change size with the seasons — they tend to swell in summer. Measure during warm conditions for a realistic fit.
  • Wider bands fit tighter. Add a quarter size when purchasing a wide ring.
  • Warm up your hands before measuring for the most accurate result.
  • Your dominant hand tends to be slightly larger. Measure the specific finger you plan to wear the ring on.
  • Do not measure when your finger is swollen from exercise, heat, or salt intake.
  • For the most precise result, use a chart that includes half and quarter sizes.

For the most accurate sizing, we recommend visiting a local jeweler who can use a professional mandrel or finger gauge. If you are ordering an engagement ring as a surprise, an inexpensive plastic mandrel can be purchased online.

The Beginning

A Diamond Visionary

Born in Turkey and trained in the art of diamond cutting, Bez Ambar arrived in Los Angeles with an idea that would reshape an entire industry. He believed diamonds could be cut differently — that brilliance didn't have to follow tradition.

In 1979, he proved it. The Princess cut was born — a square brilliant that combined the fire of a round with the modern geometry the world didn't know it wanted. It became one of the most popular diamond shapes globally.

But Bez didn't stop there.

Innovation Continues

The Blaze & Alara Cuts

The Blaze cut followed — a patented design with 13 precisely aligned facets that create an unmistakable starburst of light visible to the naked eye. No other house in the world can produce it.

Then came the Alara cut, Bez's latest creation — engineered to maximize fire and brilliance in a way that defies convention. Every Alara stone is a testament to what happens when craft meets obsession.

Three proprietary cuts. Three things no competitor can replicate. That's the Bez Ambar difference.
